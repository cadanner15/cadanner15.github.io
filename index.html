Franciscan St. Francis Health

In 2014, an article I authored was published in the employee newsletter of Franciscan St. Francis Health in Indianapolis, IN, which was accessed by over 1,000 employees. A copy of the article is visible below:

Especially during the winter months, specialists at the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) at Franciscan St. Francis Health see an increase in the number of individuals with Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD), a mood disorder commonly referred to as the “winter blues.” Symptoms of SAD are similar to symptoms of Depression, including fatigue, decreased concentration, and a sense of hopelessness. SAD, which is more common in females than in males, stems from reduced production of serotonin, a calming neurotransmitter.

Christian Ellis-Frederick, who brings 15 years of social work experience to her role as manager of the EAP, encourages St. Francis employees to speak with a specialist when facing the seemingly unbeatable “winter blues.”

“If you can’t seem to get past your low energy, decreased concentration, lack of motivation or hope, and negative feelings, please call us so we can work with you to get back on track,” said Ellis-Frederick. All Franciscan Alliance employees and dependents are encouraged to take advantage of this employee benefit. Six free EAP sessions are offered to each staff member per year. When needed, employees are referred to affiliates of the EAP or other behavioral health professionals for continued care.

“Even the best of us can get a little down or feel the effects of SAD,” said Ellis-Frederick. “At the EAP we are holding strong to the fact that spring is on the horizon.”

Ellis-Frederick suggests that employees look at a “tickler” (in this case, an online countdown) to spring, linked here: http://www.timeanddate.com/countdown/generic?iso=20140320T1257&p0=64&msg=Countdown+till+Spring+2014

To contact Christian Ellis-Frederick (LCSW) directly, call (317) 528-7900.
